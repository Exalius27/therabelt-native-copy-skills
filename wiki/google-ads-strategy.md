## Core Principle: Campaign Type Maps to Awareness Level

Google Ads has five campaign types for direct response advertisers. Which type you use depends on where your prospect sits on the Schwartz awareness spectrum — the same principle that governs [[meta-ads-strategy]] ad format selection.

**High-awareness prospects (most aware + product aware)** are already in buying mode. They know you, they're searching for you or your product category. Campaign types: **Search, Shopping, Performance Max.** These are high-ROAS, low-volume channels. They subsidize the account's overall performance.

**Low-awareness prospects (solution aware, problem aware, unaware)** need to be interrupted, educated, and moved through a belief chain before they'll buy. Campaign types: **Demand Gen, Performance Max.** These are high-volume channels that reach up to 90% of internet users. They drive scale.

## The Five Campaign Types

### 1. Search Ads
- **Format:** Primarily text ads with extensions
- **Where they appear:** Google search engine results page (SERP), search partners
- **Best for:** Most aware and product aware prospects who are actively searching
- **Characteristics:** High buyer intent, high ROAS, limited volume
- **Typical spend share:** ~11% of DR ad accounts

### 2. Shopping Ads
- **Format:** Click-to-buy product listings with image, price, and merchant name
- **Where they appear:** Google Shopping, SERP, Google Images, Maps, search partners
- **Best for:** Product aware prospects comparing options, ready to purchase
- **Characteristics:** Very high ROAS for physical products, lowest volume
- **Typical spend share:** ~4% of DR ad accounts (but additional shopping volume runs through Performance Max)

### 3. Display Ads
- **Format:** Responsive image and text ads
- **Where they appear:** Google Display Network (partner websites)
- **Best for:** Broad awareness, retargeting
- **Note:** As of April 2025, much of Display's functionality has been absorbed into Demand Gen campaigns

### 4. Demand Gen (The Dominant Campaign Type)
- **Format:** Responsive image ads, text ads, and/or video ads
- **Where they appear:** YouTube, Discover, Gmail, all Google properties, Google Display Network (as of April 2025)
- **Best for:** Cold traffic acquisition — solution aware, problem aware, even unaware prospects
- **Characteristics:** Highest volume, largest reach, drives scale. Approximately half video, half image (varies by account/market). Some advertisers dominate entirely with images, others with video.
- **Typical spend share:** ~58% of DR ad accounts
- **Critical shift (April 2025):** YouTube conversion campaigns were phased out as a standalone campaign type. All conversion-oriented YouTube ads now run under Demand Gen. Display Network inventory also opened up within Demand Gen. This made Demand Gen the single most important campaign type for DR advertisers.

### 5. Performance Max
- **Format:** Combination of all ad types (search, shopping, display, video)
- **Where they appear:** Across all Google properties and networks — Google's AI decides placement
- **Best for:** Scaling winners across all surfaces simultaneously
- **Characteristics:** You load your best-performing ads from each campaign type; Google's AI decides who sees what, where. For physical products, approximately half of pmax volume is shopping feed ads.
- **Typical spend share:** ~27% of DR ad accounts

## Spend Distribution (Post-April 2025)

Based on approximately $5 billion in spend across multiple DR ad accounts:

| Campaign Type | % of Spend | Volume | ROAS |
|--------------|-----------|--------|------|
| Demand Gen | 58% | Highest | Lower (cold traffic) |
| Performance Max | 27% | High | Mixed (AI-optimized) |
| Search | 11% | Low | Highest (buyer intent) |
| Shopping | 4% | Lowest | Very high (physical products) |

**Key insight:** Search and Shopping are low-volume but high-ROAS. They subsidize the overall account performance. Demand Gen and Performance Max drive the volume and scale. A healthy DR account uses both — high-ROAS channels fund the cost of scaling through high-volume channels.

## Paid Search: Deep Dive

### Cardinal Rule: Give Them What They Asked For

Paid search is fundamentally different from interruption platforms like Meta. On Meta, you interrupt someone and must earn their attention. On search, the prospect already told you what they want. Your job is to give it to them — not redirect, not educate about something else. If they searched for bananas, give them bananas.

This makes search the highest-intent, highest-ROAS channel. But it also means the messaging must be precisely matched to the search query.

### Four Keyword Intent Tiers

Keywords map directly to Schwartz awareness levels. Each tier requires a different messaging strategy:

| Tier | Awareness Level | Example Keywords | Messaging Strategy |
|------|----------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Brand** | Most aware | "Ray-Ban," "Ray-Ban coupon code," "[your brand] reviews" | Leverage brand loyalty + pricing. Show you're the official source. Make the offer. |
| **Product aware** | Product aware | "Ray-Ban vs Maui Jim," "Are Ray-Bans worth it," "Ray-Ban for small faces" | Proof, testimonials, fit/style guides, guarantees, risk reversal. They're on the fence — push them over. |
| **Solution aware** | Solution aware | "Best sunglasses," "polarized sunglasses," "stylish sunglasses for men" | Educate + introduce your brand as the superior option. Category searches — they don't know you yet. |
| **Problem aware** | Problem aware | "Eye strain outdoors," "glare while driving," "squinting in sunlight" | Empathize with pain, agitate, introduce your product as the solution. Low volume but cheap clicks. |

**Problem unaware** is not targetable through search — people don't search for problems they don't know they have. Use Demand Gen or Performance Max for top-of-funnel awareness.

### SERP Competitive Research Method

The best way to write paid search ads is to search your own target keywords first and study the competitive landscape. This beats writing ads blind every time.

**Process:**
1. Pick 2-3 keywords from each intent tier
2. Search them on Google and study every competitor ad on the page
3. For each competitor, note: their offer, their angle, their extensions, what they're missing
4. Find gaps: What is nobody offering? Common gaps include instant access, risk reversal/guarantees, celebrity/authority endorsement, speed/convenience, free trial
5. Write your ad to fill the strongest gap — differentiate specifically against what's already on the page

**Example — "guitar lessons" search:** All competitors offered at-home lessons with a teacher. Nobody offered instant digital lessons, a free first lesson, a well-known musician instructor, or a money-back guarantee. An ad offering all four would likely dominate the page.

**Example — "best greens powders 2025" search:** Advertorial-style ads ("I tested 18 greens powders so you don't have to") outperformed branded ads like AG1. For category/solution-aware searches, appearing impartial and editorial gets a better response than leading with your brand.

### Ad Format and Extensions

**Responsive search ad structure:**
- Up to 15 headlines (30 characters each)
- Up to 4 descriptions (90 characters each)
- Start with strong headlines, build out over time as you find winners
- Google's AI will test combinations and optimize

**Ad extensions (use all of them):**
- **Site links:** Direct links to specific pages (collections, offers, categories)
- **Callouts:** Short benefit statements
- **Call extensions:** Phone number for direct contact
- Extensions take up more SERP real estate → higher CTR → higher position → lower CPC

**Dynamic keyword insertion:** Automatically inserts the user's search term into your headline. "10-minute workout plans" when they search that, "at-home workout plans" when they search that. Ensures the ad always mirrors what the user typed.

### The CTR Virtuous Cycle

Higher CTR → Google gives you higher ad position → higher position gets more clicks → you pay less per click. This means ad quality (relevance, copy, extensions) directly reduces your cost. Invest in CTR optimization before increasing bids.

### Quiz Funnels in Search (V Shred Example)

V Shred runs search ads for "workout plans" that introduce a body-type education angle: "3 body types, 3 different diets. Take this quiz to find yours." This works because it gives the searcher what they want (a workout plan) while adding curiosity and self-segmentation. The quiz becomes the landing page, which segments prospects and serves personalized recommendations. Effective for solution-aware category searches where you want to differentiate through personalization.

## Demand Gen: Deep Dive

### Cardinal Rule: You Must Interrupt

The opposite of search. Demand Gen prospects are not looking for you — they're watching YouTube, reading Gmail, browsing the web, scrolling Discover. Your ad must stop them in their tracks. This is the same interruption paradigm as [[meta-ads-strategy]], applied to Google's surfaces.

### Image Ad Best Practices

**Big text in images outperforms clean images.** Treat your image ads like YouTube organic thumbnails — the creators who fight hardest for clicks have dialed in what works: big bold text, bright colors, faces with expressions, drama/intrigue in the image itself.

**Elements that grab attention (from YouTube organic thumbnail analysis):**
- Large, bold text with the core message
- Bright, high-contrast colors
- Human faces (especially with expressions — surprise, intensity, curiosity)
- Before/after contrasts
- Drama or intrigue that makes you want to know more

**Upload all asset dimensions.** Square, widescreen, tall — for both images and videos. Missing dimensions means missing inventory. AG1 example: only uploaded square image, got blank space on both sides in widescreen placements. Looked amateur next to organic content.

**Test images with and without text.** Some GDN placements don't allow text in images. Images with text generally perform better, but images without text access more inventory. Run both and let data decide.

**Before/afters are allowed on Google.** This is a significant policy advantage over Meta, which restricts before/after imagery in many categories (fitness, health, beauty). On Google, before/afters are fully permitted and often highly effective. Use this where Meta won't let you.

### Video Ad Thumbnail Strategy

**In-feed placements don't autoplay.** On YouTube feed, Discover, and many GDN placements, video ads appear as a thumbnail + headline. If your thumbnail doesn't earn the click, nobody ever sees your video hook. A great video behind a weak thumbnail is a waste.

**Thumbnail requirements:**
- Treat it like a standalone image ad — it must grab attention by itself
- Same rules as image ads: big text, bright colors, faces, drama
- Don't just use a frame grab from your video — design a purpose-built thumbnail
- V Shred example: great 60-second quiz video, but the thumbnail was just "somebody who fits the ideal outcome" — not compelling enough for in-feed

### Headline and Description Strategy

**Fill all 5 headline and 5 description slots, but only with strong copy.** Google's AI mixes and matches combinations. Low-performing headlines still get traffic and drag down overall ROAS. Every slot should contain copy you'd be proud of.

**Use curiosity or specificity:**
- Strong: "Stop yo-yo dieting for good" / "Superhuman memory in 31 days" / "Six things you didn't know would happen when the police arrived"
- Weak: "Try our plan" / "Brain exercises for memory" / "Simply guitar, learn guitar"

The strong headlines create intrigue, make a specific promise, or introduce an information gap. The weak ones just describe what the product is.

### Targeting Options

| Targeting Type | What It Does | Best For |
|---------------|-------------|----------|
| **In-market audiences** | Behavioral — Google knows who's actively shopping in a category | Reaching buyers with demonstrated intent |
| **Custom segments** | People who recently searched specific keywords | Getting demand gen ads in front of people with search-like intent |
| **Customer lookalikes** | Upload customer list or use pixel data | Finding more people like your best buyers |
| **Placement targeting** | Specific websites or YouTube channels | Contextual relevance (fitness product on bodybuilding sites) |
| **Demographic targeting** | Age, gender | Use with caution — large "unknown" category due to logged-out users |

Most traffic comes from mobile devices. Design for mobile first.

### Starting Strategy: One Format First

Don't launch image and video demand gen campaigns simultaneously when starting. Pick whichever format your team has stronger capabilities in. Get it working profitably, then expand to the other format. Both image and video access large inventory independently — you don't need both to get significant volume.

### Research Tool: AdBeat

Use AdBeat (or similar tools) to research competitor display ads before creating your own. Look for ads running at high volume over long periods — those are proven performers. Study their images, headlines, and messaging for patterns. This is the Demand Gen equivalent of SERP research for paid search.

## Shopping Ads: Deep Dive

### What Shopping Ads Are

Product listing ads: image + price + merchant name. Physical products only. Served on Google Shopping tab, search results, search partners, and (via Performance Max) YouTube, Gmail, GDN. Buyers make decisions based on how the product looks and what it costs — minimal copy involved. This is the Google equivalent of shelf space at a major retailer.

### Requirements

- **Google Merchant Center account** required
- **Landing page must be approved** — price and buy option must be front and center. VSL-only pages won't get approved, but adding visible product/price/buy elements to a VSL page can work.
- **Physical products only.** Info products and services cannot run shopping ads directly. Workaround: create a physical product and offer the info product as a bonus, then funnel buyers into the same back-end ascension path.
- **Product feed** is a spreadsheet with image, price, merchant name, title, and description for each product. Google dynamically matches products to searchers.
- **70%+ of shopping traffic comes from mobile.** Design and optimize for mobile-first.

### The Real Strategy: Shopping + Performance Max

Standard shopping campaigns alone deliver high ROAS but limited volume — roughly 15-20% of a well-optimized physical product account's revenue. The breakthrough comes from uploading your shopping feed into Performance Max campaigns.

**Why this works:** Performance Max uses Google's AI to serve your product listings across all Google surfaces (search, shopping, YouTube, Gmail, GDN) based on who Google predicts is ready to buy. This unlocks shopping inventory that standard campaigns can't reach.

**Setup:** Run standard shopping campaigns for baseline high-ROAS performance. Simultaneously upload the same shopping feed into Performance Max for expanded reach and volume. The standard campaigns handle the most predictable bottom-of-funnel traffic; Performance Max finds additional buyers across the network.

### Product Feed Optimization

**Images — the highest-leverage test variable:**
- Test lifestyle photos (product on a person/in use) vs. white background vs. bright colored backgrounds
- Colored backgrounds (bright yellow, green, red) make products jump off the page in a feed of white-background competitors
- In a shopping feed, all competitors are visible side by side — visual differentiation is critical

**Titles — test three approaches:**
| Title Style | Example | When It Wins |
|------------|---------|-------------|
| Simple | "Ray-Ban Aviators" | Clean, recognizable brand searches |
| Keyword-rich | "Polarized Ray-Ban Aviators" | Discoverability for specific attribute searches |
| Aspirational | "Best Aviators of 2025" | Higher CTR for category/comparison searches |

Keyword-rich titles are the default recommendation for discoverability. But aspirational titles can drive higher click-through rates in competitive categories.

**Descriptions:** Lead with your marketing message (benefit, differentiator, offer). Then stuff all relevant keywords at the end. More keywords = more chances to show up for relevant searches. This is an SEO-style approach within the product feed.

**Ratings and reviews:** Third-party review stars and counts display beneath your product listing. Products with 4-5 stars and high review counts significantly outperform unrated products. Prioritize collecting reviews on Google-connected platforms.

**Negative keywords:** Filter out irrelevant searches to avoid wasted spend on people who would never buy your product.

**Promotions:** Use Google's built-in promotion features (sales, discounts, free shipping) — these display as badges on your listing and increase CTR.

### Competitive Research in Shopping

Search your target keywords and study the shopping feed. Note:
- What images are competitors using? (Most use white backgrounds — opportunity for colored)
- What price range dominates? (Differentiate by going premium or value)
- What products are missing? (Category gaps you could fill)
- Which listings have ratings vs. don't? (Ratings advantage is real)

## Performance Max: Deep Dive

### Core Concept: PMax Is a Scaling Campaign, Not a Starting Campaign

Performance Max aggregates your proven winners from all other campaign types and lets Google's AI optimize placement across every Google surface. It is not where you discover what works — it's where you scale what already works.

**The correct sequence:**
1. Run Search, Shopping, and Demand Gen campaigns individually
2. Identify winning ads in each campaign type (highest spend, best ROAS)
3. Port those winning assets into a Performance Max campaign
4. Set targeting signals, add shopping feed, let it run
5. Result: ~27% additional volume on top of your individual campaign performance

**Why not start with PMax:** Without proven assets, you're feeding unvalidated creative into an AI optimizer. The AI has nothing to optimize toward. You'll burn budget while it tries random combinations of unproven ads across all surfaces.

### Asset Loading: Quality Over Quantity

PMax accepts massive asset volumes:
- Up to 15 images
- Up to 5 videos
- Up to 5 headlines (30 characters)
- Up to 50 long headlines (90 characters)
- Up to 5 descriptions
- Business name, logo, CTA text
- Product/shopping feed
- Automatically includes site links and extensions

**More proven assets = better performance.** But the keyword is "proven." Loading weak or untested ads dilutes results because Google will serve some traffic to every asset. Only upload ads that have already demonstrated success in their native campaign type.

You don't have to use every format. If you only have strong images and no video, that's fine — run with what's proven. Add formats as you develop winners in those formats.

### Shopping Feed Is the PMax Power Multiplier

For physical products, approximately **half of Performance Max spend goes to shopping placements.** Including your shopping feed always produces a significant performance boost. This is because shopping placements capture the highest-intent, bottom-of-funnel buyers — and PMax can serve your products to them across surfaces that standard shopping campaigns can't reach.

Without a shopping feed, PMax still works (search, display, YouTube, Gmail, Discover, Maps) but loses its most efficient conversion channel.

### Google's AI Advantage: Signals You Can't Manually Target

PMax's effectiveness comes from Google analyzing real-time behavioral data that no manual targeting setup can replicate:
- Live search queries (someone just searched for your product category)
- Browsing behavior across the web
- App usage patterns
- Location and time of day
- YouTube video watch history
- Past conversion patterns from your account

Google builds predictive models from this data and serves your best ad to the person most likely to convert, at the moment they're most likely to convert. This is why PMax consistently adds incremental volume that other campaign types miss.

## Platform Comparison: Google vs. Meta

| Dimension | Google Ads | Meta Ads |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Primary strength | Intent capture (search) + massive reach (demand gen) | Interruption-based discovery |
| Awareness sweet spot | All levels via different campaign types | Problem aware through most aware |
| Volume driver | Demand Gen (58% of spend) | In-feed video + static ads |
| High-ROAS channel | Search + Shopping (branded/product keywords) | Retargeting + most-aware audiences |
| Testing focus | Ad copy + landing page + bid strategy | Hooks, images, caption copy |
| AI role | Performance Max (full automation) | Advantage+ (contested for small accounts) |

Both platforms use the same underlying principle: match the ad format and selling intensity to the prospect's awareness level, and match the destination page length to how much selling the ad already did. See [[meta-ads-strategy]] for the Meta-specific version.
