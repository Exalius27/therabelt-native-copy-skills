# RMBC II: Reimagined Direct Response Marketing System

The complete direct-to-consumer marketing system taught by Stefan Georgi in RMBC II (2025). A holistic framework uniting research, mechanism design, brief development, and AI-assisted copy generation across all funnel stages.

## Core Philosophy

**Copywriting doesn't exist in a vacuum.** The shift from RMBC I to RMBC II: You're a copy *thinker*, not a copywriter. AI handles the heavy lifting; your job is strategic thinking—understanding product-market dynamics, building mechanisms, and coaching AI outputs to perfection.

The system compresses what once took weeks into hours. A skilled practitioner can generate:
- 2-4 high-quality VSLs in a single day
- 5-10 advertorials per hour
- 20-40 winning ads per day (Meta)
- Complete email sequences in 10 minutes

## The Five-Stage Pipeline

### Stage 1: Research (Product-Market Awareness)
[[product-market-awareness-research]] — Deep analysis of product positioning, customer desires, competitor landscape, and psychographic drivers. Three documents (PMAs, competitor research, psychographics) unified into one master research brief.

### Stage 2: Mechanisms
[[unique-mechanisms]] — Discoverable ideas that explain WHY the solution works. Generated from research using LLM ideation and a proven evaluation framework. Examples: "The 7-second bowel ritual," "The 30-second breakfast hack."

### Stage 3: The Brief 2.0
[[rmbc-ii-brief]] — A 25-point strategic document that feeds to LLMs and humans alike. Covers audience, pain points, promised transformation, mechanism, product details, story framework, pricing, objections, credibility builders, and hooks.

### Stage 4: Copy Generation
[[rmbc-ii-copy-generation]] — LLM-powered creation of all copy types using Claude projects:
- Long VSLs (20-50 minutes; rarely used now)
- Medium VSLs (6-18 minutes)
- ADHD-hook VSLs (3-7 minutes; most effective format)
- Short video ads (1-2 minutes)
- Advertorials (short and long)
- Product pages
- Meta ads
- Email sequences

### Stage 5: Funnel Architecture
[[rmbc-ii-funnel-structure]] — Strategic decisions about which copy types go where and why. Determined by 15 factors (product type, pricing, market awareness level, platform, psychology) that predict optimal funnel shape.

## Platform-Specific Context

**Context is king.** The same product sells differently on different platforms because users arrive with different intents and behavioral modes.

### Meta
[[rmbc-ii-meta-ads]] — Short video ads (often the VSL format) designed for feed placement. Can hook aggressively because ads are expected. Flow: Ad → Advertorial OR VSL → Product page → Checkout.

### Native Advertising
[[rmbc-ii-native-ads-to-advertorials]] — Ads designed to look like news articles. Users expect editorial content. Flow: Native ad (looks like news) → Advertorial (reads like article) → VSL or Product page → Checkout. Maintains congruence throughout journey.

### Video Sales Letters (VSL)
[[rmbc-ii-vsl-architecture]] — Evolving format. Classic long-form (on landing page) now rare; in-feed VSLs more common. Three sub-formats: long (20-50 min), medium (6-18 min), ADHD-hook (3-7 min). All follow hero's journey + mechanism reveal + social proof + offer.

### YouTube, TikTok, Google
Separate frameworks by platform intent. YouTube users seek education/entertainment; copy should match. TikTok demands native format and speed. Google Shopping integrates product discovery with checkout.

### Advertorials
[[rmbc-ii-advertorials]] — Long-form copy disguised as editorial. Purpose: build belief and desire before sending to VSL or product page. Short (200 words) and long (2000+ words) versions; choice determined by next step in journey.

### Product Pages (PDPs)
[[rmbc-ii-product-pages]] — Range from minimal (product + price + FAQ + checkout) to long-form mini sales letters. Structure determined by market awareness: unknown market needs long persuasion; known market needs quick checkout.

## The Journey: Two Paths

### Path 1: Prospect to Purchaser (A → B)
How unaware prospects become customers. Varies by platform, product, and market awareness:
- **Unaware market**: Ad → Long advertorial → VSL → Checkout
- **Problem-aware market**: Ad → VSL → Product page → Checkout
- **Solution-aware market**: Ad → Product page → Checkout (minimal pre-sale copy)

### Path 2: Customer to Repeat Champion (B → C → D → E)
Post-purchase monetization through cascading offers.

**Upsells** — Immediate, high-margin offers presented at checkout or on thank-you page. Psychology: stretch order value, establish pattern of buying more.

**Abandoned Cart Sequences** — Win back nearly-converted prospects. Email 1: urgency. Email 2: social proof. Email 3: discount.

**Welcome Sequences** — Set expectations, deliver value, prime for future offers. For digital products: maximize consumption. For physical products: establish authority, manage expectations.

**Promotional Sequences** — Flash sales, holiday promotions, limited-time discounts.

**Winback & Reorder Sequences** — For repeat purchase products (supplements), timely reminders when supply runs low, plus incentives to return.

## AI Integration & the Copy Chief Role

**Quad (Claude AI) projects** are the execution engine. The process:
1. Feed brief to Quad with specific instructions (story framework, hooks to test, objections to address)
2. Get outline and structure feedback
3. Iterate: "This doesn't work," "Add more reason-why," "Tease this differently"
4. Full copy generation
5. Final tweaks and polish

Your role is director, not writer. You think; the LLM executes.

## Key Frameworks & Concepts

**The 15 Factors of Funnel Structure** — Checklist that predicts optimal funnel shape:
- Product type (physical, digital, service, high-ticket)
- Price point
- Market awareness level
- Competitive landscape
- Psychology of buyer
- Platform distribution
- Supply/supply constraints
- And 8 more variables

**Mechanism Evaluation** — LLM-powered process to select best mechanism from multiple candidates. Not intuition; systematic scoring.

**Product-Market Awareness (PMA)** — Core insight. Where is your prospect on the awareness spectrum?
- **Unaware**: Doesn't know problem exists. Need big story, problem definition, solution reveal.
- **Problem-aware**: Knows problem, seeking solutions. Pitch your solution's mechanism.
- **Solution-aware**: Shopping between similar solutions. Compete on price, guarantee, credibility.
- **Product-aware**: Your customer from before OR knows about your brand. Quick checkout.

**Congruence** — Psychological principle. Each step of the journey must *feel* continuous to the prospect. Native ad → article-style advertorial → editorial VSL maintains congruence. Native ad → aggressive sales page breaks the spell.
