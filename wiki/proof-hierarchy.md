## Core Thesis

Your promise is not a differentiator. Your proof is. Anyone can copy an advertising claim, but no one can produce the exact same results to back it up. When Hormozi audited 5 years of Gym Launch ads, 80% of top performers shared one trait: they were about proof — not education, not the offer, not Hormozi center-stage. They told one story: This. Product. Works.

Belief is not binary (believe or don't). It is a continuum from doubt to certainty. Every piece of proof moves prospects along that continuum. The closer you get them to certainty, the more they buy.

**Pro Tip: Claim your proof, don't prove your claims.** Collect your data first, then make claims the data already supports. If your proof is not compelling, fix reality — give away free services, keep improving until the truth is compelling.

## The 13-Point Proof Checklist

Each item makes proof more compelling. The more boxes you check, the stronger your proof. They are ordered as a hierarchy — each moves from "more compelling" to "less compelling."

### 1. In-Person > Virtual

Seeing something with your own two eyes beats any digital version. A live demo on their doorstep beats a virtual one. Fly your best customers to events to tell their stories — you make up the cost by selling more units.

### 2. Live > Recorded

A customer walking on stage live beats playing a video of the same testimonial. Line up live testimonial speakers during pitches. They are literally "living proof."

### 3. Raw > Processed

An unedited, down-to-earth testimonial beats a highly produced version of the same story. The video with least tampering appears most authentic and has more persuasive power. Give customers examples and structure — but don't over-edit.

### 4. Show > Tell

A before/after photo beats a verbal description. Unboxing videos work because they show exactly what to expect. Record as many client experiences as possible so breakthrough moments are already captured when they happen.

### 5. Other People Saying It > You Saying It

No one cares what you think about your product — you are the most biased person in existence. Get other people to say how much they like your stuff. Build review-collection into your customer journey.

### 6. Identical to Them > Opposite of Them

Feature people in your ads who literally look like your customers. When Gym Launch ran ads with female gym owners, they got more female gym owners. When they ran older owners, they got older owners. Match age, gender, ethnicity to your market. See also [[persuasion]] (matching testimonials to demographics).

### 7. Personal > Generic

"I was fat, then I got skinny" = boring. Specific pains the reader identifies with = powerful. You want them to think "this is me." Get customers to be specific about life before AND after transformation. Ask probing questions: "What specific things happened that drove you to change?"

### 8. Big Results > Small Results > No Results

The bigger the result, the more compelling. Some companies are built on a single result (Acquisition.com on the Gym Launch sale). Reach out to longest-tenure, biggest-result customers. Pay money, invest time — it pays dividends for years.

### 9. Newer Proof > Older Proof

If your last testimonial is from years ago, prospects assume you have not had a good result since. Always be capturing new testimonials. Set up automated collection processes. Amazon lets you sort by "most recent" — there is no "sort by oldest" because no one cares.

### 10. More Proof > Less Proof

Quality has platform limitations. Quantity compensates. A wall of 5,000+ testimonials with a CTA every 3-5 entries works. Floor-to-ceiling happy reviews in person. Baffling numbers of people stayed up all night reading an entire proof page.

**Local proof tip:** Print every 5-star Google/Yelp review. Cheap frames. Line your lobby floor to ceiling. Hard to argue with hundreds of testimonials right before their eyes.

### 11. Third Party Verification > Zero Verification

Accreditations, industry awards, certifications, public approval by a trusted brand. "You don't need to believe me, believe them." Install third-party proof at all points. Amazon reviews on non-Amazon pages. BBB endorsement. NSF certified.

### 12. Proof With Numbers > Proof Without Numbers

People have a hard time arguing with understandable numbers laid out plainly. State how you collected data and when. Use charts and graphs to visualize — the right graph is more compelling than a page of numbers.

Examples from Acquisition.com:
- "54.1% of people who start a paid community on Skool make money"
- "Average Gym Lord adds over $100,000 in profit in their first complete year"
- "Average portfolio company increased EBITDA by 3.1x in first 12 months"

### 13. Metaphors > Technical Jargon

Every business has complex mechanisms. Come up with 2-3 metaphors that connect what people don't know to what they do. "Ads are like working for commissions — you only get paid based on what you put in. SEO is more like an investment account." Good metaphors are incredibly persuasive.

## Application Chain

Proof works at every step in a business:
1. **Click** — proof in the ad gets the click
2. **Opt in** — proof on the page gets the lead
3. **Buy** — proof in the presentation gets the sale
4. **Stay** — proof after purchase confirms the decision
5. **Refer** — proof-as-experience creates word of mouth

Your proof is your biggest weapon. No matter how competitive your marketplace, proof cuts through noise. And it is one of those rare things you can never have too much of.

## Proof Collection System

Build testimonial capture into your customer journey at key milestones:
- **Sign up** — document starting point
- **Onboarding** — capture first impressions
- **30-60-90 days** — milestone results
- **Breakthrough moments** — have recording already running
- **Ask probing questions** — "What specific things happened that drove you to change?" pulls better answers than "What did you think?"
- **Incentivize** — bonuses for proactive testimonials at key milestones
