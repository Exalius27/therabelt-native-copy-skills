## Previous: Long Form VSL Swipes — 31 Health VSL Scripts (174K words)

31 long-form health VSL scripts (9-57 minutes) from major brands: ProDentim, Gundry MD (8 scripts), Dr Marty Pets (3), Miami MD (4), Steve Harvey/L'Evate You, Activated You, Meticore, Quietum Plus, and more. Two new wiki pages created from cross-brand pattern extraction.

**New pages created:** [[vsl-mechanism-architecture]], [[vsl-persuasion-toolkit]]

**Key extractions:**

1. **Every health VSL uses the same 6-step mechanism construction.** Name the problem → eliminate conventional explanations → introduce the REAL root cause (something they've never heard of) → explain why with studies/analogies → explain why nothing else works → present solution that targets the root cause. Order is invariant across all 31 scripts.

2. **The root cause reframe is the structural spine.** ProDentim: not bad bacteria, lack of good bacteria. Gundry: not diet, leaky gut from lectins. Meticore: not lack of exercise, low core body temperature. Dr Marty: not genetics, processed food toxins. Miami MD: not sun/age, broken "melanin metabolism." Steve Harvey: not aging, mitochondrial decline. Without the reframe, there is no VSL.

3. **Credibility follows a 5-move choreography in fixed order.** Credential stack → disillusionment story (why they left mainstream) → conspiracy/suppression frame (who's hiding this) → difficult case study (specific patient) → exotic/foreign discovery origin. Not every VSL uses all 5, but the order never changes.

4. **The ingredient parade has its own internal rhythm.** Each ingredient: name + nickname → specific function → study with institution name → emotional payoff. Short VSLs: 3-4 items. Medium: 5-7. Long: 8-12. The parade simultaneously builds proof, stacks value, and creates narrative momentum.

5. **Closing architecture is standardized.** Price descent (3+ anchor points above reveal) → multi-bottle justification (framed as medical necessity) → guarantee ("return empty bottles" language is universal) → crossroads close (two futures, "without" path escalates) → FAQ as compressed second sales letter.

6. **Three narrator archetypes map to different audiences.** Expert (Gundry, Dr Marty): credential-first, works when doctor IS the brand. Celebrity (Steve Harvey, Maggie Q): personal struggle first, expert introduced as helper. Everyman (Meticore's Todd Pittman): maximum relatability, foreign doctor provides expertise.
