# RMBC II: Real Funnel Case Studies

RMBC II course includes in-depth breakdowns of real products and their complete funnels. These case studies show how the 15-factor decision framework translates into specific copy choices, funnel structures, and sequencing decisions.

## Case Study 1: Dr. Clean (Household Cleaning Spray)

### Product & Market
- **Product type:** Physical consumable (cleaning spray), low price ($20-50)
- **Category:** Highly commoditized (competes with Lysol, Clorox, dollar store options)
- **Market awareness:** Solution-aware (buyers know about cleaning sprays; shopping by brand)
- **Competition:** Extreme
- **Margins:** Moderate-to-healthy

### The 15-Factor Decision

**Short funnel indicators:** Low price, solution-aware market, platform efficiency needed
**Long funnel indicators:** High competition, need differentiation, small value gap

**Verdict:** Medium-to-long funnel needed because competition is so fierce. Even though price is low, buyers have many choices. Must build belief.

### Chosen Funnel Structure

**Ad → Advertorial (long form) → VSL (medium, 8-12 minutes) → Product page (with FAQs) → Checkout**

### Why This Structure?

- **Advertorial:** Positions Dr. Clean as the "smart" choice. Uses educational angle (how typical cleaners work vs. how this one works). Addresses skepticism. Maintains editorial tone.

- **VSL:** Mechanism reveal (formula science), social proof (real testimonials from trusted sources), objection handling (is it safe for kids/pets?), offer with guarantee.

- **Product page:** Confirms choice at final moment, shows specific features/ingredients, pricing, FAQ for last-minute questions, guarantee for confidence.

### Key Copy Angles

- **Mechanism:** "The enzymatic formula" (breaks down bacteria at source, not just surface coating)
- **Differentiation:** "Hospital-grade cleaning without hospital-grade toxins"
- **Social proof:** Testimonials from moms, schools, businesses
- **Objection:** Safety for kids/pets (major concern in category)

### Results Pattern

The course notes this funnel works because:
1. Advertorial slowly builds belief (efficiency, safety, results)
2. VSL creates urgency (mechanism is special, but limited supply)
3. Product page offers final confirmation
4. Guarantee removes risk (skepticism toward new brand)

## Case Study 2: Emma (Digestive Health Supplement)

### Product & Market
- **Product type:** Physical supplement, moderate price ($30-40 per bottle)
- **Category:** Digestive supplements (moderately commoditized, many competitors)
- **Market awareness:** Problem-aware (people know they have digestive issues; comparing solutions)
- **Mechanism:** Novel (unique formulation or delivery method)
- **Repeat model:** Yes (customers reorder monthly; subscription option available)

### The 15-Factor Decision

**Short funnel indicators:** Problem-aware market, repeat business model (lower acquisition cost acceptable), moderate price
**Medium funnel indicators:** Moderate competition, novel mechanism, moderate margins

**Verdict:** Medium funnel. Fast enough to capitalize on problem-aware audience, but deep enough to explain novel mechanism.

### Chosen Funnel Structure

**Ad (short VSL format, 2-3 min) → VSL (6-8 min on page) → Checkout with upsell → Welcome sequence → Promotional sequence → Reorder sequence**

### Why This Structure?

- **Ad (in-feed VSL):** Heavy hook on the unique mechanism (fast, different approach). Drives to full VSL page.

- **VSL:** Full story (struggle with digestion, searching for solutions, discovery of mechanism), mechanism reveal, testimonials, offer, CTA.

- **Checkout upsell:** Higher-tier version or "digestive guide" digital product to increase AOV.

- **Email sequences:** Welcome sequence (quick-start tips for results), promotional (restock reminder + bonus), reorder sequences (monthly reminder).

### Key Copy Angles

- **Mechanism:** "The microbiome reset protocol" (specific, memorable, testable)
- **Differentiation:** "Works differently than probiotics" (addresses common misconception)
- **Social proof:** Before/afters, testimonials from people with similar struggles
- **Objection:** "I've tried other supplements" (address skepticism on category)

### Results Pattern

The funnel works because:
1. In-feed ad creates curiosity on unique mechanism (low friction to click)
2. VSL explains mechanism fully (builds belief in the why)
3. Upsell increases first purchase value (covers acquisition cost)
4. Email sequences drive repeat revenue (subscription model sustains business)

### Full Script Insights (from Emma Long-Form VSL Template + Emma Short VSL)

The Emma product has two complete VSL scripts in the swipe file — a full long-form template (~8,000 words, annotated with section labels) and a short in-feed version (~1,000 words, 4 minutes). Key operational findings:

**The long-form template is the best-annotated VSL in the collection.** Every section is explicitly labeled (PROBLEM OPEN LOOP, COMMON ALTERNATIVES OPEN LOOP, AUTHORITY INTRO, STAR INGREDIENT 1, PRODUCT TRANSUBSTANTIATION, etc.), making it a master blueprint for the genre. It uses 7 numbered open loops before the mechanism reveal and 7 numbered CTAs through the close.

**Same narrator (Dr. Gina Sam), same architecture, different product.** Dr. Sam also narrates the Synocell (joint pain) VSL using the identical structural template. The Emma template appears to be the "master mold" from which other scripts are cast — only the root cause (archaea vs. cytokines), enemy (gut invaders vs. chronic inflammation), and ingredient list change between products.

**The short Emma VSL is a myth-bust format**, not a mini version of the long form. It opens with debunking a TikTok creator's fiber advice, positions Dr. Sam as the correcting authority, teases the 7-second trick, and drives to her video. No ingredient reveal, no price, no offer. Pure curiosity funnel into the long-form VSL.

**Price architecture (long-form):** Retail anchor unstated (implied $500 via colleague attribution) -> $69 first reveal -> $59 "because you've made it this far" -> 3-bottle at $49/bottle -> 6-bottle at $39/bottle ($1.30/day). Multi-bottle justified by: "Archaea colonies and their eggs need 90+ days to fully eliminate."

## Case Study 3: Happy Mammoth (Women's Health Supplement)

### Product & Market
- **Product type:** Physical supplement, moderate price ($40-60)
- **Category:** Women's health (health-conscious, research-aware audience)
- **Market awareness:** Problem-aware (women know about the problem; exploring solutions)
- **Mechanism:** Novel formulation
- **Margins:** Healthy

### The 15-Factor Decision

**Medium funnel indicators:** Problem-aware, novel mechanism, healthy margins, educated audience, moderate competition

### Chosen Funnel Structure

**Ad → VSL (10-12 minutes) → Product page → Upsell → Welcome sequence → Promotional sequence → Winback sequence**

### Why This Structure?

- **Ad:** Problem-aware, so minimal pre-sale needed. VSL ad directly drives to VSL page.

- **VSL:** Story of discovery (personal struggle, research journey, breakthrough), mechanism reveal (research-backed), testimonials (from similar women), offer.

- **Product page:** Confirms choice, shows ingredients and research, FAQ, guarantee.

- **Upsell at checkout:** Higher-tier package or complementary product (e.g., hormone-supporting guide).

- **Email sequences:** Welcome (education on how to use for best results), promotional (seasonal angle), winback (for customers who lapsed).

### Key Copy Angles

- **Mechanism:** "Bioavailable nutrient complex" (speaks to research-aware audience)
- **Differentiation:** "Unlike synthetic alternatives, this works with your body's natural rhythms"
- **Social proof:** Testimonials emphasizing quality of life improvement, not just symptom relief
- **Objection:** "Is it safe during [specific life stage]?"

## Case Study 4: David Protein Bars (High-Margin Snack)

### Product & Market
- **Product type:** Physical consumable (snack bars), moderate price ($1-3 per bar, $15-30 per box)
- **Category:** Protein/health bars (commoditized but trending)
- **Market awareness:** Solution-aware (people know about protein bars; brand shopping)
- **Margins:** Very healthy (wholesale cost ~$2, retail $30 for box of 12)

### The 15-Factor Decision

**Short funnel indicators:** Solution-aware, healthy margins, repeat business model
**Short-to-medium indicators:** Moderate competition

**Verdict:** Short-to-medium funnel. Audience is ready; emphasis on convenience and premium positioning.

### Chosen Funnel Structure

**Ad (lifestyle video) → Product page (quick, benefit-focused) → Checkout with upsell → Subscription program**

### Why This Structure?

- **Ad:** Minimal pre-sale. Show product in lifestyle context (athlete eating bar, results). CTA directly to product page.

- **Product page:** Short. Benefits bullets (taste, nutrition, convenience). Clear pricing for single order vs. subscription. Guarantee.

- **Upsell:** Auto-ship discount (get bars delivered monthly at 15% off). This increases LTV dramatically.

- **Retention:** Email reminders when shipment arrives, seasonal flavors, loyalty program.

### Key Copy Angles

- **Positioning:** "Premium fuel for your fitness" (not cheap competitor product)
- **Social proof:** Testimonials from fitness influencers, athletes
- **Objection:** "I've tried other protein bars; they taste chalky" (address category skepticism)

### Results Pattern

The funnel works because:
1. Short product page respects that audience is solution-aware
2. Subscription upsell is natural (consumable product)
3. Recurring revenue model supports higher acquisition spend
4. Email sequences drive retention and referrals

## Case Study 5: Capital Syndicate (High-Ticket Coaching, $5K-10K)

### Product & Market
- **Product type:** Service (coaching/mastermind), very high price ($5k-10k initial, potential $50k/year)
- **Category:** Business/investment education (high competition, many similar offers)
- **Market awareness:** Problem-aware (people know they want more income/control, not sure how)
- **Psychological barrier:** Extreme (high price, skepticism on "gurus," fear of wasting money)
- **Repeat/expansion model:** Yes (many clients go from starter to higher tiers)

### The 15-Factor Decision

**Long funnel indicators:** Very high price, high competition, extreme trust barrier, problem-aware, high margins (can afford expensive funnel)

**Verdict:** Very long funnel. This is a high-commitment decision. Must build significant belief and social proof.

### Chosen Funnel Structure

**Ad (short VSL) → VSL (12-15 min) → Product/Sales page (long) → FAQ page → Application/Discovery call → Checkout**

### Why This Structure?

- **Ad:** Problem hook (tired of trading time for money, etc.). Drives to VSL.

- **VSL:** Story (coach's background, transformation stories from students), mechanism (specific system/framework), extensive social proof (student results, case studies), objections (is this a scam? Can it work for me?), CTA to sales page.

- **Sales page:** Long form. Detailed offer breakdown. Multiple payment plans. Extensive FAQ addressing every objection. Guarantee (60-day money back for try-before-you-commit feel).

- **Application/Discovery call:** Qualify leads and remove remaining objections in conversation. Not everyone is invited; selectivity increases perceived value.

- **Checkout:** Final commitment. Premium positioning throughout.

### Key Copy Angles

- **Mechanism:** "The [System Name] Framework" (proprietary system that explains why it works differently)
- **Social proof:** Detailed case studies with specific student results (income increases, businesses built, lifestyle changes)
- **Objection:** "This is just another guru course" (addressed by highlighting teaching methodology, student results, money-back guarantee)
- **Positioning:** Premium (not for everyone, application required)

### Results Pattern

The funnel works because:
1. VSL builds belief in the mechanism and coach credibility
2. Sales page confirms decision with detailed information and guarantee
3. Application process creates selectivity (increases perceived value)
4. Discovery call closes skeptics in direct conversation
5. Multiple payment options remove price objection

## Case Study 6: Unity Eye Serum (Skincare, $97)

### Product & Market
- **Product type:** Physical skincare, premium price ($97)
- **Category:** Anti-aging skincare (highly competitive, many claims-based)
- **Market awareness:** Problem-aware (women know about aging concerns; exploring solutions)
- **Mechanism:** Novel (proprietary formulation or delivery method)
- **Margins:** Healthy
- **Repeat potential:** Medium (skincare is consumable, but loyalty is lower than supplements)

### The 15-Factor Decision

**Medium-to-long funnel indicators:** Premium price, novel mechanism, high competition, problem-aware, healthy margins

### Chosen Funnel Structure

**Ad → VSL (8-10 min) → Product page → Upsell (premium tier or complementary product) → Welcome sequence**

### Why This Structure?

- **Ad:** Problem hook (fine lines, aging concerns). VSL ad format drives to full VSL.

- **VSL:** Before-after reveals, mechanism explanation (how formula penetrates skin), scientific backing, testimonials (visible results, emotional shifts), offer.

- **Product page:** Confirms choice, shows ingredients/mechanism summary, pricing, guarantee, FAQ.

- **Upsell:** Premium size or serum + moisturizer bundle at checkout.

- **Welcome sequence:** How to use for best results (education increases perceived value), testimonials (builds confidence), prime for replenishment reminder.

### Key Copy Angles

- **Mechanism:** "Penetration Technology" or similar (makes invisible process tangible)
- **Differentiation:** "Works at the dermal layer, not just surface"
- **Social proof:** Before-afters (skincare is visual category), testimonials emphasizing confidence/feelings, not just appearance
- **Objection:** "Does it really work?" (address skepticism via research, guarantees, visible proof)

## Case Study 7: Nooro (In-Depth Platform Example)

RMBC II includes detailed breakdown of competing VSL and advertorial approaches for the Nooro product. Key insight: same product, two different companies, two different funnel strategies.

**Funnel A (VSL-focused):**
- Ad → In-feed VSL (2-3 min) → Landing page VSL (10-12 min) → Product page → Checkout

**Funnel B (Advertorial-focused):**
- Ad → Advertorial (1500 words) → VSL (8-10 min) → Product page → Checkout

**Analysis:** Funnel A creates faster ROAS initially (shorter friction). Funnel B builds more belief (advertorial sets up VSL perfectly). Actual performance depends on audience, media spend, targeting.

### Full Script Insights (from Nooro 7-Minute VSL + Nooro Medium VSL)

Two complete Nooro VSL scripts in the collection — a 7-minute ADHD-hook version and a medium-length version (~5,000 words). Comparing them reveals how the same product adapts its script architecture by format:

**The 7-minute version uses the myth-bust open.** "Lifting your legs up is the best way to relieve edema, right? Nope. Compression socks? Really? Okay, so wrap it is. No, it's not." Three common solutions dismissed in 20 seconds, then pivots to the "second heart" (calf muscle) mechanism. Product intro at minute 3, guarantee and CTA at minute 6. No FAQ, no crossroads, no personal backstory. Just: hook -> mechanism -> product -> offer -> out.

**The medium version adds a named patient story (Sarah)** as a proxy transformation, a deeper circulatory system education section, three separate therapy explanations (compression, heat, vibration) structured like the supplement ingredient parade, and an extensive objection-handling section with the viewer's likely doubts stated in second person ("You might think it's too expensive..."). It also includes an explicit crossroads close ("Are you going to take control of your edema or let it control you?") absent in the 7-minute version.

**Device pricing architecture differs from supplements.** Official price $249.95 -> 40% discount through this link. No multi-tier descent, no per-day math. Single price point with a discount code. The 90-day guarantee gets the same "just say maybe" language as supplement VSLs.

## Pattern Recognition Across Case Studies

### Pattern 1: Price Determines Funnel Length
- Under $50: Short-to-medium funnel
- $50-500: Medium funnel
- $500+: Long funnel

### Pattern 2: Market Awareness Determines Copy Angle
- Unaware: Problem education first
- Problem-aware: Mechanism/solution differentiation
- Solution-aware: Superiority positioning

### Pattern 3: Repeat Business Model Allows Longer Acquisition Funnels
- One-time purchase: Must maximize margin on single sale → shorter funnel
- Repeat/subscription: Can invest in longer funnel → lower margin acceptable

### Pattern 4: Mechanism Novelty Justifies Longer Explanation
- Novel mechanism: 8-12 min VSL explanation needed
- Known mechanism: 3-5 min explanation sufficient

### Pattern 5: Psychological Barriers Extend Funnel
- Low barrier (commodity product): Short funnel
- High barrier (skepticism, shame, high price): Long funnel with extensive social proof
