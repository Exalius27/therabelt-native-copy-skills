## Core Thesis

Product selection is a pass/fail gate, not a creative exercise. A product must meet ALL criteria before you invest in ads, copy, or funnels. This checklist screens for products that can sustain a profitable Facebook Ads business — not just sell once, but compound over time.

This operationalizes [[halbert-market-first]]'s "starving crowd" principle into a concrete e-commerce screening tool.

---

## The 7 Criteria

### 1. Proven to Sell — $300k/mo+ Revenue via Facebook Ads

Don't guess whether a product can work. Find products already generating $300k/month or more through Facebook Ads. Someone else has already validated the market, the ad economics, and the demand.

This is Halbert's wallet test applied to e-commerce: ignore what people say they want, look at what's already selling at scale.

### 2. Solves a Problem with Urgency and Intensity

The problem must feel urgent — not "nice to solve someday" but "I need this fixed." Intensity means the person feels the problem acutely enough to act now, not browse.

Connection to [[avatar-segmentation]]: desire depth determines whether someone scrolls past or stops. Products solving urgent problems convert at lower CPAs because the prospect is already motivated.

### 3. Pricing — 2.5x to 3x AliExpress Cost (70% Gross Margin)

Sell at 2.5-3x the AliExpress sourcing price while remaining reasonable relative to competitors' pricing. This produces ~70% gross margin — enough headroom to absorb ad costs, returns, and still profit.

The margin math connects to [[offer-design]]'s AAC formula: you need enough gross profit per unit to fund acquisition costs and still hit your profit target. Without 70% margins, Facebook Ads economics break down fast.

### 4. CPA Target — $50 Recommended

A $50 cost-per-acquisition target keeps the unit economics viable. At 70% margin on a product selling for $50-60, a $50 CPA is roughly breakeven on the front end — which is sustainable only if LTV architecture (criterion 7) exists.

Connection to [[rmbc-ii-funnel-structure]]: the 15 decision factors include price point and margins as determinants of funnel length. A $50 CPA constraint pushes toward shorter, more efficient funnels.

### 5. Lightweight — Held in Two Hands

The product must be lightweight enough to hold in two hands. This avoids high shipping costs that destroy margins. Shipping cost is a hidden margin killer — a product with perfect unit economics can fail if fulfillment eats the profit.

### 6. Evergreen — Year-Round Demand

The product must sell year-round, not just seasonally. Seasonal products create revenue gaps, force you to rebuild ad accounts, and make scaling unpredictable.

Connection to [[rmbc-ii-funnel-structure]] Factor 8 (time urgency): evergreen products have "constantly urgent" demand rather than seasonal spikes.

### 7. LTV Architecture — Repeat Revenue Built In

The product must support at least one of three repeat-revenue paths:

- **Complementary products** — can be sold with other items (bundles, accessories)
- **Multiples needed** — people need more than one unit
- **Consumable** — people run out and reorder

This is the criterion that separates sustainable businesses from one-shot product launches. Without LTV architecture, you're paying full acquisition cost for every sale with no backend to fund it.

Connection to [[offer-design]]'s AAC formula: LTV from repeat purchases is what lets you lose money (or break even) on the first sale and still build a profitable business. Connection to [[rmbc-ii-funnel-structure]] Factor 10-11: repeat purchase models and natural upsells justify longer, more expensive acquisition funnels.

---

## How to Use This Checklist

Screen products against all 7 criteria before committing any resources. A product that fails even one criterion carries significant risk:

- Fails criterion 1 → unvalidated market (highest risk)
- Fails criterion 3 or 4 → ad economics won't work
- Fails criterion 7 → no path to sustainable profitability

The checklist is a minimum bar, not a guarantee. A product that passes all 7 still needs strong creative, good funnel structure, and solid execution.
