## Core Thesis

The difference between brands stuck at inconsistent sales or a $3-4k/day ceiling and brands scaling to $20-40k+/day is not talent or luck — it's feedback loops. Every ad test produces data. Without a system for capturing, analyzing, and compounding that data, you restart from zero each time a winner dies. With feedback loops, each test (winner or loser) adds to a growing body of knowledge that systematically improves your hit rate over time.

The failure mode: you find a winner, scale it, it dies, you panic, launch random tests, nothing works, blame the product, start over. This cycle repeats because you never understood WHY the winner worked. You got lucky once and mistook luck for skill. Feedback loops turn luck into skill.

## Principles

### 1. Never Launch Without a Documented Hypothesis

Before any creative goes live, you must articulate WHY you think it will work. If you can't, you're guessing randomly — and random guesses produce no learning even when they succeed.

A hypothesis must be specific:

**Bad (too vague):**
- "I think this will work because the copy is good"
- "This hook seems attention-grabbing"
- "Competitors are doing something similar"

**Good (testable):**
- "I'm testing the desire to stop snoring (helping partner sleep better) because Amazon reviews and Reddit threads in r/sleep showed 15+ comments about partner consequences ('my wife is threatening to sleep in another room'). I think this desire resonates because the external consequence (relationship tension) creates more urgency than personal discomfort."

The hypothesis specifies:
- **What variable** you're testing (desire, angle, visual, awareness level, lead type)
- **What avatar** you're targeting
- **What desire** you're amplifying
- **What angle** frames the argument
- **WHY** you believe this combination will work — grounded in research, not intuition

This is what makes post-test analysis possible. Without a hypothesis, you can't determine what worked or failed — only that the ad did or didn't perform.

**Connection:** The hypothesis draws directly from [[copy-research]] (voice of customer data, Amazon reviews, forum mining) and [[insight-vectors]] (mechanism identification). Research quality determines hypothesis quality.

### 2. Structured Post-Mortem: 3-5 Testable Hypotheses Per Result

After results come in, return to your hypothesis and ask: "Was I right? If not, why?"

Most people stop at "it didn't work" or "it worked." That's not analysis. Your job is to generate 3-5 specific, testable hypotheses about WHY.

**For losers — what failed and what to adjust:**

Each hypothesis should identify a specific failure point and propose a specific, testable adjustment:

1. Was the lead inauthentic? (Marketer voice vs. customer language) → Adjustment: Rewrite using verbatim VOC language from [[copy-research]]
2. Was the visual generic? (No pattern interrupt, no visceral detail) → Adjustment: Test a more specific, recognizable visual
3. Was the desire itself wrong? (Research pointed elsewhere) → Adjustment: Test a completely different desire angle
4. Was the awareness level mismatched? (Educating people who already know, or assuming knowledge they don't have) → Adjustment: Shift awareness level targeting
5. Was the mechanism uncredible? (Claims without reason-why) → Adjustment: Strengthen the unique mechanism with new information

**For winners — what worked and what to replicate:**

Each hypothesis should identify what to carry forward and how to build on it:

1. Did the awareness level targeting work better than expected? → Replicate: Test more ads at this awareness level
2. Did the unique mechanism create genuinely new information? → Replicate: Always introduce a mechanism that explains why previous solutions failed
3. Did antagonizing existing solutions amplify urgency? → Replicate: Frame existing solutions as villains — not just inferior, but harmful

**The discipline:** Document everything in a Learnings column. This raw data feeds Pattern Recognition (Principle 3).

### 3. Weekly Pattern Recognition Across Tests

Individual ad analysis tells you what happened. Pattern recognition across 10-20 tests tells you what's actually true about your market.

**The weekly review (four questions):**

1. **What do my losers have in common?** Look at awareness level, lead type, visual style, desire, angle, voice. What keeps appearing in the loss column?
2. **What do my winners have in common?** Same variables. What keeps appearing in the win column?
3. **What should I stop doing?** Turn loser patterns into rules. "10 of 15 losers targeted unaware audiences" → Stop targeting unaware audiences.
4. **What should I keep doing?** Turn winner patterns into rules. "4 of 5 winners antagonized existing solutions" → Always frame existing solutions as dangerous.

**From patterns to rules:**

Patterns become testing constraints that eliminate losing variables and double down on winning ones. Examples from a cat water fountain brand (real case study):

- Losers: 10/15 targeted unaware audiences (high CPC), 9/15 used generic benefit language (low conversions), 7/15 had marketer voice leads (all flopped)
- Winners: 4/5 targeted product-aware audiences (took majority spend immediately), 4/5 antagonized existing solutions (strong conversion), 3/5 used story leads with specific emotional details (lower CPAs)

Rules created: Only target product-aware. Always introduce a UMP explaining why current solution is dangerous. Every lead must use verbatim customer research language.

**Connection:** Pattern recognition reveals which [[avatar-segmentation]] sub-avatars, which [[insight-vectors]] mechanism types, and which awareness levels are working. It's the empirical validation layer for all the theoretical frameworks in the wiki.

### 4. The Compound Effect — Hit Rate Over Time

Feedback loops compound. Without them, every winner death is a reset to zero. With them, knowledge accumulates:

- **Month 1:** 5% hit rate (1 winner per 20 tests). You're building the data set.
- **Month 2-3:** 10-15% hit rate. Patterns are emerging. You create your first rules.
- **Month 4-6:** 20-25% hit rate. You've eliminated variables that consistently fail. You're doubling down on what works.
- **Month 6+:** You have a playbook. 3-5 winners per month instead of 1.

The key insight: creating winners isn't linear (1+1=2). Every test teaches something. Those insights compound. Eventually, stacked learnings produce a breakthrough. But only if you're tracking, analyzing, and reviewing.

## The Creative Tracker — Variables to Document

Every ad test requires documentation of these variables before and after launch.

### Before Launch (Hypothesis)

| Variable | What It Captures |
|----------|-----------------|
| Launch Date | When you're testing |
| Creative Type | Static image, carousel, sales letter, video, etc. |
| Type of Test | New concept or variation of winner? If variation, specify what you're varying |
| Landing Page | Which page this sends to |
| Ad Inspiration | Where the idea came from (customer research, competitor, pattern from weekly review) |
| Variable Being Tested | The specific thing you're isolating: desire, angle, visual, lead type, awareness level |
| Main Desire | The instinctual thing someone wants to achieve by experiencing your product benefit |
| Avatar | WHO exactly has this desire that your product benefit can fulfill |
| Angle | Your unique framing/argument/story that makes someone act on their desire |
| UMP | Unique Mechanism of the Problem — unknown cause of the problem / why everything else failed |
| UMS | Unique Mechanism of the Solution — what about your product uniquely solves the UMP |
| Lead Type | Story, proclamation, secret, etc. (see [[copy-structure]]) |
| Awareness Level | Where this avatar sits on the awareness spectrum (see [[rmbc-ii-research-methodology]]) |
| Hypothesis | Your specific, research-grounded guess about WHY this will work |

### After Results

| Variable | What It Captures |
|----------|-----------------|
| Status | In progress, launched, paused, killed |
| Results | CPC, CPM, conversions, ROAS |
| Learnings | 3-5 testable hypotheses on WHY it worked or failed + what to test next |

---

## The System Summarized

1. **Before launch:** Fill out the tracker. Document your hypothesis. Be specific about variable, avatar, desire, angle, and WHY.
2. **After results:** Return to hypothesis. Loser: 3-5 hypotheses on WHY it failed + adjustments. Winner: 3-5 hypotheses on WHY it worked + what to replicate. Document in Learnings.
3. **Once a week:** Review last 10-20 tests. Find patterns across losers and winners. Create rules (stop doing X, keep doing Y).
4. **Repeat.** Every test either makes money or teaches something. Both are valuable. Insights compound over 3-6 months. Hit rate improves from 5% to 25%+.
